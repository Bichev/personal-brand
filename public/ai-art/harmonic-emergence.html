For 7-Year-Olds ▼

Imagine dropping lots of pebbles into a calm pond. Each pebble makes ripples (circles in the water). When the ripples bump into each other, sometimes they make bigger waves (when they meet at the top), and sometimes they cancel out and become flat (when a top meets a bottom)!

The colorful dots you see are like tiny boats floating on these invisible ripples. They ride up and down and spin around, leaving trails that show where they've been. Different colored trails mean the boats are riding on different kinds of waves!

Physics: Wave Interference ▼

Superposition Principle: When two or more waves occupy the same space, they combine. The resulting wave is the sum of all individual waves at each point.

Constructive Interference: Wave peaks align (in phase), creating regions of high amplitude. Particles move quickly with bright colors.

Destructive Interference: Peaks meet troughs (out of phase), canceling each other. Particles move slowly or remain still.

Standing Waves: Regions of consistent high and low amplitude that appear stationary even as waves propagate.

Mathematics ▼

Sine Waves: f(x,y,t) = A·sin(ω·r + θ + φ + t·v)

  • A = amplitude (wave height)
  • ω = frequency (waves per distance)
  • r = distance from center
  • θ = angle around center
  • φ = phase offset
  • t = time
  • v = wave speed

Harmonic Series: Frequency ratios (1:2:3:4...) create natural resonance, similar to musical overtones.

Music Theory: Harmonic series (octaves, fifths, thirds) follows the same mathematical ratios.

Philosophy & Meaning ▼

Resonance: Things "in tune" amplify; discord cancels—a metaphor for cooperation.

Harmonic Emergence

An Algorithmic Philosophy

"The universe is written in the language of mathematics, and its characters are triangles, circles, and other geometric figures." — Galileo Galilei

At the intersection of mathematics and aesthetics lies a profound truth: complexity need not be chaotic, and simplicity need not be sterile. Harmonic Emergence explores the delicate balance between these polarities through the language of wave interference and resonant frequencies. This is not about simulating natural phenomena but about discovering the intrinsic beauty hidden within mathematical relationships themselves—the way sine and cosine functions, when properly orchestrated, can dance together to create patterns that feel both inevitable and surprising.

The computational heart of this philosophy rests on phase-modulated oscillators arranged in carefully calibrated layers. Each layer operates at a distinct frequency ratio, chosen not arbitrarily but according to harmonic principles that resonate with fundamental musical intervals. These oscillators don't simply exist in isolation; they interact through constructive and destructive interference, creating regions of intense activity and peaceful stillness.

The visual manifestation emerges through a field of particles, each one a witness to the invisible harmonic forces that permeate the space. These particles don't follow predetermined paths; instead, they respond to the local field intensity at their position, which is determined by the sum of all overlapping wave functions. Where waves reinforce, particles gather and glow with accumulated energy. Where waves cancel, particles drift slowly or find stillness.

Color in this philosophy is not decoration but information—a direct encoding of the underlying wave dynamics. Hue represents the dominant frequency at each location, shifting smoothly as different oscillators take precedence. Saturation maps to wave amplitude, revealing the strength of the field. Brightness encodes particle density and velocity, creating natural contrast between active and dormant regions.

The parameter space has been carefully constrained to ensure that every configuration produces compelling results while still offering meaningful variation. The number of harmonic layers determines the complexity of interference patterns. Wave speed controls the temporal evolution, allowing exploration of both dynamic turbulence and serene near-stasis. Frequency ratios can be adjusted to explore different harmonic relationships, from consonant intervals that feel stable and musical to dissonant ratios that create visual tension.

What emerges is a living algorithm—one that reveals different facets of its mathematical soul with each iteration, yet always maintains coherence and intentionality. The viewer witnesses not a static image but a computational performance, where mathematical precision gives rise to organic beauty. Each seed generates a unique instantiation of these harmonic principles, proving that order and variety are not opposites but partners in the creation of algorithmic beauty.
